Courses

Validated by: Queen’s University Belfast

Start Date: 08/09/2025

Location: Titanic Quarter Campus

Course Code: P00120-AA

Entry Requirements:

  • Applicants must be 19 years or over at the date of entry.
  • Must have been out of full-time education for at least 2 years.
  • GCSE Grade C or above in English.
  • GCSE Grade C or above in Maths.
  • GCSE Grade C or above in a Science subject.

Fees: N/A

Overview:

This one-year, full-time course at our Titanic Quarter Campus provides an alternative route for mature students applying for higher education. There is no upper-age limit, and most Access to University courses require few—if any—prior qualifications. However, a pre-course assessment may be required.

The course is validated by either Queen’s University Belfast (QUB) or Ulster University (UU). It is an intensive and challenging program that requires a high level of commitment. All Access programmes/courses are Level 3 and considered equivalent to A-levels. They are widely recognised by UK universities, with many students progressing to a variety of degree courses.

Key Areas of Study:

  • Chemistry
  • Microbiology
  • Biology

Validated by: Queen’s University Belfast

Start Date: 08/09/2025

Location: Titanic Quarter Campus

Course Code: P00120-AE

Entry Requirements:

  • Must have been out of full-time education for at least 2 years.
  • GCSE Grade C or above in English Language (or Level 2 equivalent Literacy qualification).
  • GCSE Grade C or above in Mathematics (or Level 2 equivalent Numeracy qualification).
  • *Applicants whose first language is not English should refer to the QUB International Students section under "English language requirements for international students" as different requirements apply.

Fees: N/A

Overview:

This two-year, full-time programme at Titanic Quarter Campus is ideal for those looking to undertake a degree in biological sciences, enter the bioscience industry, or pursue a health-related profession.

The course focuses on developing personal and academic confidence. Students will study Biology, Chemistry, Study Skills, and ICT (if they have not achieved a Grade C or equivalent in ICT).

This programme may include enrichment units designed to enhance employability and progression. A minimum attendance of 80% is expected.

Validated by: Ulster University

Start Date: 08/09/2025

Location: Titanic Quarter Campus

Course Code: P00300-AB

Entry Requirements:

  • a) Have attained a pass in one subject at GCSE, ‘A’ level, or an alternative Level 3 qualification approved by Ulster University, plus grades A, B, or C in three other subjects at GCSE level or an equivalent standard.
  • b) Provide evidence of competence in written and spoken English (GCSE Grade C) or equivalent.
  • OR Applicants must normally provide evidence of their ability to undertake the programme through the accreditation of prior experiential learning.

Fees:

  • Academic Period 2025:
    • HE Tuition Fee: £670.00
    • Examination / Registration Fee: £170.00
  • Academic Period 2026*:
    • HE Tuition Fee: £605.00
    • Examination / Registration Fee: £175.00

*Fees are payable annually and may be subject to change.

Overview:

The Cert HE in Combined Social & Behavioural Sciences is a two-year, Level 4 higher education course equivalent to the first year of an undergraduate degree. This course is validated by Ulster University.

Students study Sociology, Psychology, and Quantitative Research Methods in Year 1. These are core modules and are offered on both a day and evening basis. In Year 2, students choose either a Sociology or Psychology pathway and can select from a variety of optional day or evening modules.

There are no hidden material costs. However, students may need to cover subsistence costs (e.g., lunch) for any field trips or visits.
